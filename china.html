Everything you should know
 before Traveling to CHINA

Flag: The Chinese flag is a symbol of Chinese history and culture. This flag also represents the unification of the people as one nation after decades of dynasties and divisions.
CHINA is Known for:

1. Feng Shui
First of all, you should know that the Feng Shui is an ancient art and science that originated in China over 3000 years ago, and now it´s one of the most typical Chinese things. Feng means wind and Shui means water, as these two elements are always associated with good health in China.
2. The Chinese zodiac
One of the things China is famous for is the Chinese zodiac. It is also known as Shengxiao, which means birth likeness and now it´s popular all over the world. The Chinese Zodiac tends to be compared with the Western Zodiac System, and its main difference is that the Chinese zodiac correlates with 12 years instead of 12 months
3. Kung Fu
Kung Fu China is famous for its Kung Fu. The Kung Fu is famous basically because is shown in many movies, who have never heard of Bruce Lee, Jackie Chan or the most recent one Kung Fu Panda.

Best Food that you should taste once:
1. Chow mein
Chow mein are Chinese stir-fried noodles with vegetables and sometimes meat or tofu; the name is a romanization of the Taishanese chāu-mèn. The dish is popular throughout the Chinese diaspora and appears on the menus of most Chinese restaurants abroad.
2. Dumplings
Dumpling is a broad class of dishes that consist of pieces of dough wrapped around a filling, or of dough with no filling. The dough can be based on bread, flour or potatoes, and may be filled with meat, fish, cheese, vegetables, fruits or sweets
3. Spring Rolls
Spring rolls are a large variety of filled, rolled appetizers or dim sum found in East Asian, Southeast Asian cuisine. The name is a literal translation of the Chinese chūn juǎn. The kind of wrapper, fillings, and cooking technique used, as well as the name, vary considerably within this large area, depending on the region's culture.
Best Places to visit:
1. Shanghai
The biggest city in the country, Shanghai is known for skyscrapers and contemporary lifestyle in heart of a booming economy. The city is a perfect blend of modern architectural marvels and historic buildings. Aside from being a financial hub, the city is also home to massive departmental stores that sell everything from luxury fashion to high-end electronics.
2. Beijing
Capital to the world's most populated country, Beijing is known for its numerous Historical Monuments and a Well-Preserved Culture. Also, home to the Great wall of china, the city is a hub for the tourist from all around the world. Aside from a rich history, the city boasts modern architecture and a contemporary lifestyle.
3. Hong Kong
Hong Kong is widely recognized for being a shopping mecca. Visitors may also take in panoramic views of the city from a high vantage point, eat local cuisine, participate in festivals, watch sporting events, etc
4. Guangzhou
Guangzhou not only boasts some of the oldest temples in China, a millenarian-old park, an imperial tomb of a southern kingdom and complete preservation of colonial villas and church on Shamian, but it also serves as a showcase of futuristic architectures and autopilot metro on Zhujiang New Town.
5. Shenzhen
From gorgeous theme park to stunning architectural art pieces, Shenzhen has evolved from being a garden city to a metropolitan hub. Privileged as one of the first china’s social-economic zone, the city attracts people from all over the world, bringing cultural diversity and making it one of the finest business and shopping hub.
Best Season to visit
Spring and Autumn- The best times to visit China are spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October), when most of the popular places have their most tourism-friendly weather. The cultural and historical destinations like Beijing, Shanghai, and Xi’an are hardly affected by weather conditions. They are suitable to be visited all year round.
